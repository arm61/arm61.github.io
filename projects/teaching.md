The importance of data in the physical sciences cannot be overstated. 
However, frequently chemistry and physics students complete their studies without gaining the skills to manipulate datasets effectively. 
Empowering students with these skills enables a more complete modern scientist, with the skills to work either in academia or science. 

Currently, I hold a Visiting Lectureship at the University of Bath Department of Chemsitry, where along with [Dr Ben Morgan](https://morgan-group-bath.github.io) I organise the [final year computational chemistry module](https://pythoninchemistry.org/ch40208/welcome.html), focused on traning students to utilise Python programming for chemical applications.
In response to the COVID-19 pandemic, this module work transitioned to fully online/remote learning using a flipped learning approach. 

In addition to directly training students, I have lead in the development of a series of tools to help learners (at all levels) understand the role of computational modelling in the physical sciences. 
This includes the [pylj](https://doi.org/10.21105/jose.00019) package for visualising a simple two-dimensional classical atomistic simulation and the [The interaction between simulation and scattering](https://doi.org/10.1107/S1600576719004333) module developed as part of the pythoninchemistry project.
