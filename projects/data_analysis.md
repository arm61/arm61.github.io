The analysis of experimental data is fundamental to the physical and chemical sciences. 
However, data analysis is rarely a trivial task, frequently being the most time-intensive part of a research project. 
The use of data science methods, including Bayesian inference and machine learning, can increase experimental throughput, improve analytical reproducibility, enable greater integration of theory, and possibly allow for a more complete understanding of the experimental technique.

The application of data science methodology is currently being used to [improve the interpretation of neutron reflectometry measurements](https://doi.org/10.1088/2632-2153/ab94c4) and to develop [statistically informed approaches to quantify diffusion in battery materials](https://kinisi.readthedocs.io).
Additionally, we have developed [software tools to make the use of Bayesian analysis more accessible](https://uravu.readthedocs.io) to those in the physical sciences.
