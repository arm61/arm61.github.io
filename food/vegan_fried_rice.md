## Ingredients (2 portions)

- 180 g Japanese rice (in the UK this is usually sushi rice in the supermarket but support and independent asian market)
- Some frozen peas
- Tofu block (get the Calderon one that is in water)
- A carrot
- An onion (color not important really)
- A head of broccoli
- 4/5 cloves of garlic
- Agave syrup (a wee bit)
- Soy sauce (eyeball it)
- Optionals: Sesame oil, rice vinegar, kimchi, fresh coriander

## Recipe

1. First thing is to cook the rice. Wash the rice like three of four times then add 1.3 times the mass of rice you have of water. So for 180 g rice it is 234 g water. Put it on the stove on high with no lid on and when the water comes just to the boil, turn it down to a low, put the lid on and set a 15 minute timer and don’t touch it. It should click away gently. After 15 minutes, take of the lid and with a spoon move things around for a bit, then put the lid back on for like 5 or 10 minutes to stem. Rice fucking done.
2. Chop the broccoli, carrot, onion, and garlic (you can do the latter three in the food processor if you are feeling lazy).
3. Cut the tofu into small cubes and mash it with the back of a fork, it might be possible to do this in the food processor but I have never tried.
4. Oil a pan and fry the chopped veg for like 8 minutes, then add a small amount of water and stick a lid on the pan, this will stream the broccoli a bit.
5. Add the tofu, rice, soy sauce and agave syrup (and any of the optionals) and cook for 10 minutes. Potentially add some water if things look like they are dying out.

## Tip

Whenever you make rice, make double the amount you need and then you will have spare rice in the fridge for when you wanna quickly make vegan fried rice! (the internet says rice will keep in the fridge for like 3 days if you let it cool properly. google this and you shouldn’t die).
